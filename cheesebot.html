The Community Health Line

Increasing Health Program Awareness in Tillamook County

A Proposal by Samuel Edwards

The Challenge: An Awareness Gap

Tillamook County offers vital health services, but residents often don't know they exist.

  • Key barrier to better health outcomes is awareness, not availability.
  • Critical areas affected: Diabetes, Opioid Support, and Maternal Health.
  • Current communication methods don't reach everyone effectively.

The Solution: A Community Health Line

A bilingual (English/Spanish), automated SMS text-messaging service.

It feels like a helpful community bulletin board, not a complex "AI".

Key Benefits

  • Meets residents on a platform they already use and trust.
  • No app to download, no website to navigate.
  • Provides proactive alerts and on-demand information.

How It Works

A simple process for both residents and the Health Department.

(Use the down arrow to see the flow for each group)

How It Works (For Residents)

Step 1: A resident subscribes to a topic.

Resident Texts: JOIN

System Responds: Welcome! Reply with a topic: 1. DIABETES, 2. OPIOID, 3. MATERNAL, 4. ALL

How It Works (For Residents)

Step 2: They can ask for info at any time.

Resident Texts: SCREENING

System Responds: Free diabetes screening next Tue, Aug 31 at the Community Hall from 9am-2pm. Walk-ins welcome.

How It Works (For the Health Dept)

Designed for zero daily management.

  • Access to a simple, secure web dashboard.
  • Fill out a simple form to add new events or alerts.
  • Schedule alerts to be sent to specific subscriber groups.
  • The system handles all resident interactions automatically.

Measuring Success

The goal is a 20% increase in participation at health events.

We can track key metrics to measure impact:

  • Higher turnout for screening events.
  • Increase in calls to program hotlines.
  • Number of residents signing up for text-based reminders.
